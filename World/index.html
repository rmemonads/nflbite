What is the World Cup?

The World Cup, organized by FIFA, is the most prestigious football tournament in the world. Held every four years, it features national teams from all continents competing for the coveted trophy. The tournament showcases legends and rising stars from teams such as Brazil, Argentina, Germany, France, England, Spain, and Italy.

History of the World Cup

The first World Cup took place in 1930 in Uruguay. Since then, the tournament has grown in size and popularity, producing memorable moments, legendary goals, and unforgettable matches. Countries like Brazil, Germany, and Italy have dominated historically, while emerging teams continue to challenge the giants.

World Cup Format

The tournament usually follows a two-stage format:

  • Group Stage: Teams are divided into groups, playing round-robin matches.
  • Knockout Stage: The top teams from each group advance to elimination rounds including Round of 16, Quarter-Finals, Semi-Finals, and the Final.
